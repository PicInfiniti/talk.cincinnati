Talk Cheat Sheet

Isomorphism in Union-Closed Sets

Presenter: Mohammad Javad Moghaddas Mehr
Event: Cincinnati – March 2025

🔹 Opening

Hello everyone! Thanks for being here.
Today, I’ll talk about Isomorphism in Union-Closed Sets, which explores the structure of union-closed families via isomorphisms.

🔹 Background: Frankl’s Conjecture

In any finite union-closed family, some element appears in at least half of the sets. (Frankl, 1979)

It feels intuitive, but remains unproven for over 40 years. We’ll build from this idea.

🔹 Main Question

If two union-closed families are isomorphic, do corresponding elements have the same frequency?

Isomorphism: A bijection that preserves union operation between two families.

🔹 Counterexample & Insight

Not all elements preserve frequency under isomorphism. But some do.
This led to identifying redundant elements: elements that don’t affect family size or structure when removed.

🔹 Key Definitions

  • Redundant Element: Removing it doesn't change family size.
  • Reduced Collection: A family after removing a redundant element.
  • Pure Family: A family with no redundant elements.

🔹 Purification Process

Redundant elements are removed iteratively to get a 𝒦*, the purified version.
Different purification orders may result in different forms, but they’re isomorphic.

🔹 Revisiting Our Question (for Pure Families)

With purification, we can now ask:

In pure union-closed families, is frequency preserved under isomorphism?

🔹 Main Results

  • Theorem: Isomorphic pure families preserve element frequencies.
  • Corollary: Set operations (union, intersection, etc.) preserve cardinality under isomorphism.
  • Hyperisomorphism: A bijection at the element level that induces a family-level isomorphism.
  • Theorem: Every isomorphism corresponds to a hyperisomorphism.

🔹 Conclusion

  • Any union-closed family can be purified without losing structure.
  • The frequency behavior is now well-understood in the pure case.
  • We also proved a weaker form of Frankl’s conjecture in related work.
